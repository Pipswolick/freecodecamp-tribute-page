Roger Zelazny

Hugo and Nebula award–winning author of fantasy and science fiction

Here's a time line of Roger Zelazny's life:

  • 1937 - Born in Euclid, Ohio
  • 1954 - First professional publication in literary magazine for fantasy short story "Mr.Fuller's Revolt."
  • 1959 - Graduated Western Reserve University with a B.A. in English.
  • 1962 - Graduated Columbia University with an M.A.. Wrote a thesis on humor and morality conventions in the 1600's Revenger's Tragedy.
  • 1962-1969 - Worked for the U.S. Social Security Administration. Wrote stories at night, progressing from short stories to novelletes to novellas to full-length novels. Zelazny's first novel-length work was in 1965.
  • 1967 - Published Lord of Light, which follows a struggle between immortals who wield advanced technology to act as gods.
  • 1969 - Quit job to become fulltime writer.
  • 1970 - Published Nine Princes in Amber, the first entry in the Amber Chronicles series. In it, an amnesia patient discovers that he is Corwin, a prince from another world called Amber, and he is embroiled in a deadly conflict with his brothers for the throne.
  • 1995 - Died in Santa Fe from kidney failure associated with cancer.
  • Influence - Authors who have credited Zelazny as an inspiration in their own writing include George R.R. Martin, Samuel R. Delaney, Neil Gaiman, and Andrzej Sapkowski.
